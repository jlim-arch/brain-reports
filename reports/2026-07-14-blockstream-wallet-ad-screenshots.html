The reference set is a tight, repeatable formula — bold all-caps headline + straight-on phone mockup + a floating 3D coin/badge motif + a background color tied to the action on screen. That formula ports cleanly to a Blockstream set. The one thing that doesn't port directly: three of the four reference panels sell buy/sell/swap trading (custodial-style convenience), which isn't Green's product — Green is a self-custody Bitcoin/Liquid wallet built around Jade hardware pairing and multisig, not an in-app trading venue. Rather than invent trading screens Green doesn't have, the brief below swaps those panels for Green's real value props (self-custody, Liquid assets, Jade pairing, multisig backup) and flags it as an open question — confirm which product (Green vs. Jade Plus vs. both) before a designer starts.

The reference, panel by panel

Panel 1 — Brand + portfolio

Cream/off-white background. Logo lockup top-left. Bold black headline, two lines, tight tracking: "EFFORTLESSLY MASTER CRYPTO." Straight-on phone mockup right side showing a portfolio screen — total balance, % change in green, an asset list with icons (BTC/ETH/USDT/BNB/SOL), and a Send/Receive/Buy/Swap action row. A glossy 3D Bitcoin coin floats bottom-left, overlapping the mockup edge for depth.

Panel 2 — Price chart + social proof

Background transitions cream to black (gradient). Phone mockup shows a price chart with timeframe tabs (Today/24H/7D/1M/1Y), an orange "BUY BITCOIN" CTA pill, and a wallet-balances list below. A dark ribbon banner reading "100M+ USERS" with a laurel icon sits at the bottom of the frame — social proof as a design element, not just copy.

Panel 3 — Buy/sell, stacked mockups

Solid black background. Bold white headline: "BUY, SELL INVEST SAFELY." Two phone mockups stacked with a slight offset — top one on a mint-green card showing a Buy flow ($250 input, teal CTA), bottom one on a lavender card showing a Sell flow ($100 input, purple CTA). Color-coding the action (green = buy, purple = sell) is the key device here.

Panel 4 — Swap, single mockup

Solid brand-orange background. Bold white headline: "SWAP & TRADE IN A FLASH." Single phone mockup showing a full swap interface — You Pay / You Receive fields, live rate, fee breakdown (provider, network fee), and a numeric keypad with a blue "SWAP" CTA. Busiest screen of the four, placed last.

The reusable formula

  • One action per panel. Each screenshot sells exactly one thing — never a feature dump.
  • Headline is 3–5 words, all caps, tight tracking, high contrast against the background. It states the outcome ("effortlessly master crypto"), not the feature name.
  • Background color is coded to the action: neutral/cream for the brand-intro panel, black for the trust/safety panel, brand-orange for the highest-energy panel (swap/trade). Consistent within a set of 4, not random per panel.
  • Phone mockup is straight-on, no perspective tilt, cropped tight so the screen content reads at thumbnail size in the App Store carousel.
  • One floating 3D/glossy asset per set (coin, badge) as a recurring brand motif — used once or twice, not on every panel, so it doesn't compete with the CTA.
  • CTA button color inside the mockup matches the panel's semantic meaning (buy = green/teal, sell = purple, swap = blue, primary brand action = orange) — carries through even inside the simulated UI.

What it means for us

Open question — confirm before a designer starts: is this for Green (the app-store wallet, direct visual analog to the reference), Jade Plus (hardware device — would need a different mockup device than a phone screen), or both as a combined set? The brief above assumes Green since it's the only Blockstream product that's actually an app-store listing with screens to mock. If the ask was really "make Jade Plus ads that look like this," the phone-mockup format doesn't transfer as-is and needs a product-shot-based version instead.

Handoff notes for a designer or AI generator (Midjourney/Figma): reuse Blockstream's existing brand orange and type system from jade-plus-landing/ad-dashboard-fluid rather than the reference's exact palette — the formula (headline + mockup + coded background + one floating motif) is what's worth copying, not Bitcoin.com's specific colors. Real UI screenshots from the actual Green app should be used for the phone-mockup content, not invented UI — none of the screens above should be fabricated pixel-for-pixel without pulling from the live app.
