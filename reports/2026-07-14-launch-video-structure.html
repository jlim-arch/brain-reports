Bottom line

→
Great launch videos aren't feature reels — they're a five-beat argument: Hook → Problem → Solution/reveal → Benefit demo → single CTA, run in 60–90 seconds. The failure mode most brands fall into is starting with company context ("today we're excited to announce...") instead of the hook — viewers decide whether to keep watching in the first 3 seconds, before they care who you are.

The five beats

BeatTimingJob
Hook0–3 secState the payoff or problem immediately, or show the product mid-solve. No logo, no preamble.
Problemnext ~10 secBrief amplification of the pain point — makes the viewer feel understood, not lectured.
Solution / revealmidProduct enters as the direct answer to the problem just named.
Benefit demomid–lateOne key benefit, shown in a real-use-case context — not a feature tour.
CTAcloseOne specific next step. Not three, not "learn more" — one.

Why this structure wins

Tone over feature count

The best launch videos read as confident and emotionally resonant, not exhaustive. The goal isn't to explain the whole product — it's to create enough desire that the viewer clicks through to learn more themselves. Cramming in every feature dilutes the hook and drags the video past the point where view-through rates hold up.

Length is a constraint, not a preference

60–90 seconds is long enough to complete all five beats, short enough that drop-off doesn't eat the CTA. Below that, the problem/solution beats get rushed; above it, hero-placement view-through starts declining fast.

What it means for us

This maps directly onto Blockstream product/campaign launch videos (wallet features, mining hardware, Liquid updates):

  • Kill the announcement-first open. Any script draft that opens with "Blockstream is excited to announce..." should get the first line cut and replaced with the hook — the problem or the payoff, cold.
  • One CTA, chosen before the script is written. Decide whether the ask is "download the app," "read the docs," or "sign up" up front — don't let the edit default to stacking two or three.
  • Demo one benefit, not the feature list. For custody/wallet launches this means picking the single use-case that best proves the headline benefit, rather than a feature-by-feature walkthrough.
  • Budget for 60–90 sec as the hero cut, with a :15 hook-only cutdown for paid social — same structure, beats 1–2 only.
