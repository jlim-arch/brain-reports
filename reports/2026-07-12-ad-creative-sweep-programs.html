Flagging a false premise first: nothing in the industry is actually branded a "creative sweep program" — searches turn up generic "creative testing" and "creative refresh" methodology, not a named category. What John is likely picturing is the workflow those tools support: continuously rotating and refreshing ad creative before it fatigues. The category splits into three layers that no single tool covers end to end — research/briefing (Foreplay), test design and post-launch analysis (Motion, Marpipe), and rules-based automation (Revealbot, Madgicx) — plus a separate generation layer (AdCreative.ai) for producing variants at volume. We already run FB+Google reporting and adset analysis manually (ads-manager-fluid); the highest-leverage build is a fatigue-detection + rotation-rule layer on top of what we have, not a from-scratch creative-testing suite.

The three layers, and who owns each

Research & briefing

Foreplay — swipe-file tool. Saves competitor ads from Meta/TikTok/LinkedIn into boards, screenshots landing pages, tracks ad activity status. Its "AI Briefs" turns saved inspiration into creative briefs/storyboards. "Spyder" does 24/7 competitor ad-library scraping with hook export. $49–$99/mo.

Test design & analysis

Motion — post-launch analytics. Doesn't design the test, but gives element-level insight into which concepts are winning inside the live ad account. Marpipe plays a similar role for experiment design. Best paired with a briefing tool upstream.

Rules-based automation

Revealbot — "Top Creatives"/"Top Audiences" tracking, bulk ad creation across variants, and a rules engine for budget scaling, pausing underperformers, and scheduling. Reports pushed to email/Slack. Madgicx overlaps here: pre-set + custom automation rules, "Smart Filter" segmentation by funnel stage/country/device, and a briefing-to-designer pipeline (24h turnaround on requested assets). $39–$99/mo.

What it means for us

We already do the manual version of the analysis layer — Mon/Wed/Fri FB+Google reports, 7d/30d window checks before touching a campaign. The gap is upstream (systematic swipe-file/briefing) and downstream (automated fatigue detection + rotation), not the analysis itself.

Best features to build into our own program

  • Fatigue detection, not just reporting. The category consensus is ads fatigue in 3–5 days with CTR dropping 20–40% by day 7. A simple rule (CTR/CPA delta vs. rolling 3-day baseline, triggered per adset) into the existing dashboard would catch this before John has to eyeball it.
  • A swipe-file board, scoped to bitcoin/crypto + fintech advertisers — Foreplay's core idea, but narrow and internal rather than a paid general tool. Feeds creative briefs without a subscription.
  • Rotation rules on top of ads-manager-fluid — Revealbot/Madgicx's rules engine is the most directly portable idea: pause-under-threshold, promote-winner, schedule-based rotation, logged and pushed to a dashboard card (not Slack, per standing preference).
  • Skip the generation layer for now. AdCreative.ai-style bulk static generation solves a volume problem we don't have yet — worth revisiting only once briefing + rotation are running and creative throughput becomes the bottleneck.
