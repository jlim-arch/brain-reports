Bottom line

→
Fireblocks and Blockstream aren't direct substitutes. Fireblocks sells generalized multi-chain custody infrastructure (MPC key management, a 150+ chain network, a regulated NY trust charter) to banks who need to support many assets at once. Blockstream is bitcoin-only end to end — mining, Liquid Network, Bitcoin treasury advisory, and open-source multisig hardware (Jade) — which is a narrower but more defensible position for institutions whose mandate is specifically "hold bitcoin, minimize custodial surface area." Our wedge is bitcoin-native purity and self-custody/collaborative-custody optionality, not feature parity on multi-asset infrastructure. One flag: we couldn't confirm the "Fireblocks raised another round" premise from the capture — no 2026 funding round shows up in search, only partnership expansions (see below).

Company snapshots

Fireblocks

Multi-chain digital asset infrastructure platform. Pioneered the MPC-CMP protocol, splitting key shares across the client, Fireblocks, and an independent recovery partner. Because MPC operates at the cryptographic (ECDSA/EdDSA) layer, one implementation secures wallets across all supported chains at once — bitcoin is just one of 150+.

Live in production with BNY Mellon, Revolut, Galaxy, and 80+ banks. The Fireblocks Network connects 2,400+ institutional counterparties and settles $70B+/month in self-custody, on-chain transactions. Fireblocks Trust Company is chartered by the NY Dept. of Financial Services for qualified custody.

No new funding round found in 2026 — last disclosed raise is the $550M Series E (Jan 2022, $8B valuation). 2026 activity has been partnership expansion: a Thales tie-up for bank-grade HSM security (Feb 2026) and Canton Network integration for regulated tokenization/settlement (Feb 2026).

Blockstream

Bitcoin-only, vertically integrated: mining colocation, the Liquid Network (a bitcoin sidechain securing $3B+ TVL and 19,000+ BTC bridged), Blockstream AMP for asset issuance, and Blockstream Enterprise — a platform for corporates and governments to manage treasury/balance sheet via Liquid, with FIX/REST integration into custodians and exchanges.

On the wallet/custody side: Blockstream Green (self-custody, singlesig or 2FA multisig, "Multisig Shield" splits one key to the user's device and one to Blockstream infra) and Jade, an open-source hardware wallet used in collaborative-custody products like Swan Vault (2-of-3 multisig — customer holds two keys across two Jade devices, Swan holds a recovery key).

Raised $210M in Oct 2024 (led by Fulgur Ventures) explicitly to grow Layer-2 adoption, expand mining, and grow Blockstream's own corporate Bitcoin treasury — one of the largest publicly known.

What it means for us

We don't win by matching Fireblocks feature-for-feature on multi-chain MPC infrastructure — that's their entire business and they have a multi-year head start plus a regulated trust charter and bank-scale sales motion. The wedge is positioning against the thing Fireblocks structurally can't claim: bitcoin is one of 150+ assets to them; it's the only asset to us.

Where that argument lands

  • Strategic bitcoin reserves / corporate treasuries with bitcoin-only mandates — reduces the case for paying for a general multi-chain custody platform when the mandate never touches the other 149 chains.
  • Sovereignty-sensitive institutions — post-FTX, "not fully custodial" still sells. Collaborative custody (2-of-3 multisig, customer holds majority control) via open-source Jade hardware is a different trust model than handing keys to a platform, even a regulated one.
  • Full-stack bitcoin story — mining, treasury, Layer-2 settlement (Liquid/AMP), and custody-adjacent hardware in one vertically integrated company is a narrative Fireblocks can't tell; they are infrastructure-only.

Where we're structurally behind

  • No regulated trust charter equivalent to Fireblocks Trust Co. — institutions that need that specific wrapper go elsewhere or through a partner (as Swan does for Vault).
  • Institutional network effects: 2,400+ counterparties and 80+ live banks is a large moat we can't close by out-marketing; the counter-positioning has to be "different tool for a different mandate," not "cheaper Fireblocks."
